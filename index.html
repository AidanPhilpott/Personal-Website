About Me

Who am I? What can I do? What are my goals?

I am an imaginative interaction designer born and raised in Toronto, Ontario. I have always been very interested in technology and design. I built my first website when I was 11 and my first computer when I was 14. Now I want to help create things for other people.

I am well versed in the Adobe Creative Suite. My favourite programs inside it include Illustrator, Photoshop, and Adobe XD. I am also am experienced in Framer X and Sketch. I enjoy learning new programs and expanding my skills.

I occasionally like to play around with 3D design and animation. I am experienced in HTML and CSS, and I know the basics of JS and JQuery. I am a creative designer who loves to focus on the UX side of websites and physical products.

My goal is to learn from the top UX/UI minds in the world so that I can contribute solutions that improve life.

My Process

  • Explore The Situation
  • Collect Data
  • Ideate
  • Execution
  • Adapt and Improve

Before I begin any brainstorming, I need to explore the situation at hand. I analyze all of the data that I have access to and create a strategy.

It is essential to understand the needs of the business that is creating the product, the people who will be using it, and the people who will be building it before I launch into the planning of the design.

Research is my friend. My next step is to research the product, the competition, the users, other designers, and much more. I gather as much information as possible.

This data will be used to create a design statement and a problem statement. These will help define the path that I want to take with the project. The data will help me understand the target users so I can make a product that fits their needs.

This step is all about brainstorming. This is where the research and thoughts turn into ideas. I use brainstorms and mind maps to present my thought process. I use a sketchpad to jot down any and all ideas that come to mind.

After the brainstorming has concluded, it is time to review the concepts that have been created. This stage includes the reviewing of ideas, the evaluation of ideas, and ultimately, the tossing of ideas. In the end, the best ideas will remain. These ideas lay the foundation for a whole new round of brainstorming. This process continues until there is a final polished concept.

This is when the concept comes to life.

It is always a good idea to work smarter rather than work harder. During this stage, a decision has to be made. I decide what tools will be used to get the job done. Many tools can be used to illustrate a concept but my favourites are Framer X, Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, and good old-fashioned pen and paper. The concept is what dictates the strategy for its creation and the tools that will be used along the way.

No design is perfect and variables are always changing. The iteration phase involves opening the concept to be critiqued and eventually tested. The information that is generated from this stage fuels the evolution of the concept. The product will evolve and become better as more information and critiques are generated.
